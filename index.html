1. Guruvayur Temple

Guruvayur Sri Krishna Temple is a pilgrimage town situated in Thrissur, the southwest Indian state of Kerala and is one of the famous temples in Kerala. It is a renowned Hindu temple devoted to the Hindu deity Lord Krishna known as Lord Guruvayurappan. This temple town of Guruvayur is a popular destination for worshippers in Kerala and all over India. The temple follows a series of customs and traditions that captivates lakhs of pilgrims every month. The Father or Lord of Guruvayur is portrayed and represented as a four-armed God showing his supreme powers and immense strength to carry out countless acts at a single time. The central icon represents the lord Vishnu carrying the conch Panchajanya, the discus Sudarshana Chakra, the mace Kaumodaki and a lotus with a Holy basil garland. Here is Kerala famous temple list that are absolutely worth visiting if you are in search of peace.Being one of the most important and sacred pilgrim centres of Kerala, it is known as the Dwarka of South India.

2. Sabarimala Sri Ayyappa Temple

Sabarimala Sri Ayyappa Temple is the most well known and prominent one among all the Sastha temples in Kerala. It is one of the famous temples in Kerala. Deity Ayyappa is believed to be the son of Shiva and Mohini (the feminine incarnation of Vishnu). This shrine is situated on a hilltop surrounded by mountains and dense forests. It is approximately 4000 feet above sea level in Pathanamthitta district, Kerala. Sabarimala supports the values of equality, fraternity, and also permanence to human life. It has commonly been assumed that the Prince of Pandalam dynasty, an epitome of Ayappan, meditated at Sabarimala temple. The Pathinettu thripadikal or the 18 sacred steps is the primary stairway to the temple. The joy of pain and happiness (Swamiye Saranam Ayyapa) gathers uniqueness in the voice of all worshippers, thereby signifying all men are equal before Lord Ayyappan regardless of class, creed, or race. Above 30 million devotees pay a visit to Sabarimala annually making it the largest pilgrim destination in India. The shrine is open on the first 5 days of each Malayalam month.

3. Kaviyoor Mahadeva Temple

Kaviyoor Mahadeva Temple is one of the ancient and important Kerala temples located on the banks of the river Manimala. The main deity is Lord Shiva (also known as Thrikkaviyoorappan). It has got a unique architecture with its copper-covered roof, golden flag masts, and the wide steps at the eastern entrance. The outer wall of the holy place is bedecked with wooden sculpture panels. This Kerala famous temple is of historical importance and preserved as a monument by Archaeological Department. The Idols of Ganapathy, Maharshi, and Dwarapalakas are also worshipped within the temple premises. Restrictions are placed on a non-Hindu from making an entry to the temple. Strict dress code is followed and photography is completely prohibited.

4. Mammiyoor Temple

Mammiyoor Mahadeva Kshetram, representing the deity Shiva is known for its astonishing paintings on the walls and magnificent sculptures. Immediately after visiting the Guruvayur Temple, the devotees pay a visit to the Mammiyoor Temple too. The Mammiyyoor temple is the famous temple in Kerala and also has the sanctums of Lord Ganapathi, Mahavishnu, Darma Sastha, Muruga, Bhagavati, and Brahmmarakshas. The Idol of Bhagavathi depicting Parvathy has been erected behind the idol of Lord Shiva. Mammiyoor temple is a part of 108 famous Shiva temples of Kerala.

5. Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple Trivandrum

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is one of the most respected abodes of Hindu Deity MahaVishnu situated in Trivandrum, capital of Kerala and is considered as the richest temple in the world . Being the famous temple in Kerala, the architecture of the temple exhibits a mix of Keralan and Dravidian styles. The shrine has the idol of Lord Mahavishnu which is 18-feet high and is an awe-inspiring view for the devotees who visit the temple. 6. Attukal Bhagavathy Temple

Attukal Bhagavathy Temple is a religious shrine at Attukal, in Kerala. The main deity worshipped here is Goddess Bhadrakali. Bhadrakali is regarded as the goddess of prosperity and protecting her devotees from any harm. This Famous Kerala temple shows the confluence of traditional Tamil and Kerala styles of architecture. The temple is quite famous for the annual Attukal Pongala Festival. Millions of women participate during this Attukal Pongala, and this festival has entered into the Guinness book of world records. Attukal Devi is frequently worshipped in 3 forms such as Maha Saraswati( Goddess of knowledge), Maha Lakshmi (Goddess of wealth) and Mahakali/Durga/Parvathy (goddess of power).

7. Vadakkunnathan Temple Thrissur

Vadakkunnathan temple is one of the ancient and the most famous Hindu Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The shrine is situated in the city of Thrissur, a state in Kerala. This famous temple in Kerala has mural paintings and the architectural style depicts the mythological tales from Mahabharata. This is one of the oldest Kerala temples spread over a large area of 9 acres. This is regarded as the first temple built by Parasurama, the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu. Lord Shiva is the deity present in the Vadakkunnathan Temple. During Shivarathri the temple is lit up with lakhs of lamps.

8. Ambalapuzha Sri Krishna Temple

Ambalapuzha Sri Krishna Temple, located at Ambalapuzha in Kerala is one of the renowned Kerala temples. This shrine is dedicated to Lord Krishna is one of the famous temples in Kerala. An embodiment of Krishna as a child resides in the temple. The deity of this shrine has been brought from Guruvayoor during the time of Tipu Sultans conquest. Payasam, a sweet pudding prepared with rice and milk is served in the temple. It is also reckoned that Lord Guruvayoorappan pays a visit to the temple daily to accept the offering.

9. Chottanikara Devi temple

Chottanikara Devi temple is a well-known and popular temple worshipping the Goddess Bhagawati. The temple is known for its curing powers. This famous temple in Kerala is situated in the suburbs of Kochi and built in a typical Kerala architecture using wood primarily. It is believed that the Deity Bhagawati removes all mental illnesses and disorders, thereby resulting in peace and harmony in the life of her devotees. A golden idol of the Goddess resides in the temple.

10. Vaikom Mahadeva Temple

Ettumanoor- Vaikom-Kaduthuruthy Temple which is dedicated to the Deity Shiva is also popularly known as Vaikom Mahadeva Temple. Considered as one of the oldest and the most famous temples of Kerala, the Shiva Linga is believed to be from Treta Yuga. It is strongly believed that those who visit the temple get their desires fulfilled and their inner soul becomes pristine and pure. Its beautiful architecture and meticulously designed murals also play a major role in capturing the attention of a large number of visitors.
